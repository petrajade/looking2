Looking 2 Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 7, 2016

This privacy policy governs your use of the mobile application Looking 2 (the "Service") for mobile devices that was created by Looking 2, Inc. (the "Company"). You are encouraged to read this Privacy Policy carefully and in conjunction with the Terms of Use.


How The Company Uses Information Collected

The Company uses the information collected about you to deliver and improve product and services and manage our business; provide customer support; perform research and analysis about your use of, or interest in, our or others' products, services, or content; communicate with you about products or services that may be of interest to you from the Company or third parties; develop and display advertising tailored to your interests on our Service and other sites; analytics; enforce any rights under our Terms of Use; and perform services as described to you at the time of collection.

User Provided Information

The Service obtains the information you provide when you download the Service and create an account.


In order to register as a user with Looking 2, you will be asked to sign in using your Facebook or Google login. If you do so, you authorize the Company to access certain Facebook or Google account information, including, but not limited to your public Facebook or Google profile (consistent with your privacy settings), your email address, interests, likes, gender, birthday, education history, relationship status, current city, photos, personal description, friends, Facebook URL or contact lists.


If you use the Service to chat with other Users, you provide the Company with the content of your chats. If you contact the Company with a customer service request, or for any other reason, you provide the Company with that information.


The Service may deliver notifications to your phone or mobile device. You can disable notifications by logging out of the Service, deleting the Service, or by changing the settings on your mobile device.


You may choose not to provide the Company with certain information, however that may result in you being unable to use some or all features of the Service as such information may be required in order for you to create an account; purchase products or services; or initiate other transactions.


When you create an account and use the Service, you may be asked to provide transaction-related information, such as when you make purchases, respond to any offers, or download or use applications from the Company and/or credit card information for purchase and use of the Service.


The Company may also use the information you provide to contact you from time to time to provide you with important information, required notices, and marketing promotions.

Automatically Collected Information

In addition, the Service may collect certain information automatically, including, but not limited to, the type of mobile device you use, your mobile device's unique device ID, your IP address, your mobile operating system, the type of mobile Internet browsers you use, and analytic information about how you use the Service.

Accessing Your Information

You have the ability to review and update your personal information with the Service by opening the mobile application and going into the Service settings. You may also delete your account at any time by visiting the Settings page. If you delete your account, the Company will retain certain information associated with your account, further described below.

Real Time Location Information

While using the Service, the Company may use GPS technology (or other similar technology) to determine your current location in order to determine the city you are located within and display a location map with relevant advertisements or offers. The Company will not share your current location with other users or partners.


If you do not want the Company to use your location for the purposes set forth above, you should turn off the location services for the mobile application located in your mobile phone settings and/or within the mobile application.

The Company will share your information with third parties only in the ways that are described in this privacy statement.


The Company may disclose User Provided and Automatically Collected Information:


  1. as required by law, such as to comply with a subpoena, or similar legal process;

  2. when the Company believes in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect its rights, protect your safety or the safety of others, investigate fraud, or respond to a government request;

  3. in connection with corporate transactions, including but not limited to, sales, mergers, acquisitions, or consolidations; and/or

  4. with the Company’s trusted services providers who work on our behalf, do not have an independent use of the information the Company disclose to them, and have agreed to adhere to the rules set forth in this privacy statement.

The Company works with advertisers and third party advertising networks, who need to know how you interact with advertising provided in the Service. These third parties may also obtain your non-precise location information in order to help analyze and serve anonymous targeted advertising on the Service and elsewhere. Nothing in this Privacy Policy is intended to restrict the Company’s use or sharing of aggregated or de-identified information in any way.


This Privacy Policy does not create rights enforceable by third parties or require the disclosure of any personal information relating to users of the website.

Opt-Out Rights

You can stop all collection of information by the Service easily by deleting your account. You may use the standard uninstall processes as may be available as part of your mobile device or via the mobile application marketplace or network.


Your California Privacy Rights: Under California’s "Shine The Light" law, California residents have the right to ask companies once a year what personal information they share with third parties for those third parties’ direct marketing purposes.


If you would like to ask for more information under the Shine the Light Law, you can send your request to 1770 Green Street, #501, San Francisco, CA 94123.


When you send your request, use the phrase "California Shine the Light Request" so that the Company may identify your question, and include your mailing address, state of residence, and e-mail address so that the Company may provide a response.
